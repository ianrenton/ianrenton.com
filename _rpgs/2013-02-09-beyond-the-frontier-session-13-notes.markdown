<h2 style="text-align: center;">Story</h2>
&nbsp;

Leveru uppu!

&nbsp;
<h4>Last time, on Dungeons and Dragons...</h4>
After the King dispatched Ailsa back beyond the portal, the party tooled up and accompanied the king on his quest to find his son. They battled their way into the lava tunnels surrounding the city as the green foliage slowly started to die back. In the tunnels, they encountered some Atar wielding a terrifying new weapon...

&nbsp;

&nbsp;
<h4>In the Tunnels</h4>
The tunnels continue for some way, then where the King thinks the party should carry on, there is an abrupt end to the corridor. Party finds a way through, only to have it reform behind them.

Past it, they reach the top of a long spiral staircase heading down. It gets hotter and hotter towards the bottom.Â And just before they reach the bottom... it falls away, revealing bubbling lava below, and the wizard.

The lava is illusory. At one point the wizard will summon a golem from the walls.

&nbsp;

Once dispatched, the lava vanishes and an imposing doorway is revealed.

&nbsp;
<h4>The Showdown</h4>
Inside the door, an imposing scene -- Lord Granite sits on a throne even more impressive than the King's. He is flanked by guards, and in the background, wizards are clustered around a glowing fire. As the party enters, the wizards move to flank Granite, the fire dying as they do. But Granite stands, and waves them back.

"An odd choice of company you have chosen to keep, Father."

"One might say the same of you."

They argue, resulting in Granite suggesting that perhaps it is time for a <em>new</em> king.

And roll initiative. Granite holds the others back.

&nbsp;
<h4>The Aftermath</h4>
Granite lies defeated. His soldiers back away or swear fealty once again to their king. The wizards make themselves scarce. The king takes an amulet that Granite wore round his neck -- or a character does. They return to the palace.

"Under normal circumstances I am sure that it would be a time for celebration," says the King. "However, I must retire to... think about what has occurred today. You are free to stay, or leave according to your whim. The amulet will, when presented to the portal, ensure that it returns you to your world."

The party at last returns to their world, stepping through the fiery portal back to the Forestborn enclave in their own world. Behind them, the fire of the portal flickers... but remains blazing. Sparks flicker and flash, and dry wood starts to catch alight.

The Queen turns, no easy feat for her.

"Guards!"

&nbsp;

End session.
